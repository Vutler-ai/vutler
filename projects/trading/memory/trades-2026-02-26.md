### Thesis Post-Mortem
**Was thesis correct?** NO. Completely wrong.

**What went wrong:**
1. **Overestimated momentum**: Assumed $100k breakout was imminent, but price reversed violently
2. **Ignored volatility risk**: Crypto can move -30% in hours; position sizing should have been smaller
3. **Headline trap**: "Bitcoin surges to $96,500" was actually the TOP, not the start of a rally
4. **No confirmation**: Should have waited for price to hold above $95k for 48h before entering

### Lesson Learned
**When crypto shows parabolic move (+5-10% in a day), wait for consolidation before entering. Buying the top of a spike = high risk of immediate reversal. Next time: require 48h price stability above key level before committing capital.**

Morning portfolio value: €9,762 (includes the BTC loss already realized)
Evening portfolio value: €11,746
**Day gain: €1,984**

But that's because the morning portfolio.json had **outdated prices**. The morning snapshot showed QQQ at €523.37, but that was actually YESTERDAY'S price. Today's close is €520.22, which is actually DOWN €3.15/share.

**Real day movement:**
- QQQ: -€3.15/share * 4.76 = -€15
- EXS1: +€1.28/share * 7.81 = +€10
- GLD: -€1.69/share * 4.96 = -€8

The big +€1,746 total gain is from QQQ's run-up YESTERDAY (Feb 25), not today.

**Key insight:** I'm still learning to distinguish between:
1. **Position P&L** (entry → now) = +€1,746 total
2. **Daily P&L** (yesterday close → today close) = -€13 today

Today was a consolidation day. Winners held gains. That's healthy after yesterday's rally.

**Lesson:** After a big up day (+23%), a flat/small down day is normal and healthy. Don't confuse "not crashing" with "not performing."
