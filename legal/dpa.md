## 2. Scope and Application

### 2.1 Scope of Processing
This DPA applies to all Personal Data processed by Starbox on behalf of Customer in connection with the provision of Vutler services.

### 2.2 Hierarchy of Documents
This DPA supplements and forms part of the Service Agreement. In case of conflict, this DPA takes precedence for data protection matters.

### 2.3 Duration
This DPA remains in effect for the duration of the Service Agreement and until all Personal Data is returned or deleted.

## 3. Data Processing Details

### 3.1 Categories of Data Subjects
- Customer's employees and authorized users
- Customer's clients and business contacts
- End users of Customer's Vutler workspace

### 3.2 Categories of Personal Data
- **Identity Data**: Names, email addresses, user IDs
- **Contact Data**: Email addresses, phone numbers, organization details
- **Professional Data**: Job titles, department information, role assignments
- **Communication Data**: Chat messages, file uploads, conversation history
- **Technical Data**: IP addresses, device information, usage logs
- **Authentication Data**: Login credentials, session tokens, access permissions

### 3.3 Purpose of Processing
Personal Data is processed solely to:
- Provide Vutler platform services
- Maintain platform security and performance
- Provide technical support and troubleshooting
- Generate anonymized usage analytics
- Comply with legal and regulatory requirements

### 3.4 Retention Period
Personal Data will be retained for the duration specified in Starbox's Privacy Policy unless Customer provides alternative written instructions.

## 4. Processor Obligations

### 4.1 Processing Instructions
Starbox shall:
- Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer
- Immediately inform Customer if instructions violate Applicable Data Protection Law
- Not process Personal Data for own purposes beyond service provision

### 4.2 Confidentiality
Starbox ensures that persons authorized to process Personal Data:
- Are bound by confidentiality obligations
- Receive appropriate data protection training
- Have access only to data necessary for their role

### 4.3 Security Measures
Starbox implements appropriate technical and organizational measures including:

**Technical Safeguards:**
- Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
- Multi-factor authentication for administrative access
- Regular security assessments and penetration testing
- Automated backup and disaster recovery systems
- Network segmentation and access controls
- Intrusion detection and monitoring systems

**Organizational Safeguards:**
- Data protection by design and by default
- Regular staff training on data protection
- Background checks for personnel with data access
- Incident response procedures and data breach protocols
- Vendor management and due diligence processes
- Annual security audits and compliance assessments

### 4.4 Sub-Processor Management
Starbox may engage sub-processors for data processing, subject to:
- Written agreement imposing equivalent data protection obligations
- Customer consent (general authorization with notification)
- Right for Customer to object to sub-processor changes
- Processor remains fully liable for sub-processor performance

- Notify Starbox immediately of data incidents

## 6. International Data Transfers

### 6.1 Transfer Mechanisms
For transfers to countries without adequacy decision:
- **EU Standard Contractual Clauses** (2021/914/EU)
- **Swiss Standard Contractual Clauses** (as updated)
- **Additional safeguards** as required by applicable law

### 6.2 Transfer Risk Assessment
Starbox conducts transfer impact assessments considering:
- Legal framework in destination country
- Practical access by public authorities
- Additional safeguards to mitigate risks
- Alternative transfer mechanisms if needed

### 6.3 Customer Rights
Customer may:
- Request copies of transfer mechanism documents
- Require additional safeguards for specific transfers
- Object to transfers that pose high risks
- Request data localization for sensitive categories

## 7. Data Subject Rights

### 7.1 Assistance Obligations
Starbox shall assist Customer in responding to Data Subject requests for:
- Access to Personal Data (Article 15 GDPR)
- Rectification of inaccurate data (Article 16 GDPR)
- Erasure of data (Article 17 GDPR)
- Restriction of processing (Article 18 GDPR)
- Data portability (Article 20 GDPR)
- Objection to processing (Article 21 GDPR)

### 7.2 Response Timeframe
Starbox will respond to Customer requests for assistance within 10 business days or as required to meet Customer's legal deadlines.

### 7.3 Technical Assistance
Starbox provides technical measures to facilitate Data Subject rights through:
- Self-service data export tools
- Account deletion functionality
- Data rectification capabilities
- Access logs and audit trails

## 8. Data Protection Impact Assessment

### 8.1 Assistance Obligation
Starbox shall assist Customer with Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) by providing:
- Information about processing activities
- Security measures and safeguards
- Risk mitigation strategies
- Processing records and documentation

### 8.2 Consultation Rights
Where required, Starbox will assist with regulatory consultations and provide necessary documentation to supervisory authorities.

## 9. Records of Processing Activities

### 9.1 Processing Records
Starbox maintains records of processing activities including:
- Categories of processing carried out for Customer
- Data transfers to third countries
- Security measures implemented
- Sub-processor arrangements
- Data retention and deletion schedules

### 9.2 Audit Rights
Customer may:
- Request processing records relevant to their data
- Conduct audits with reasonable notice (max once per year)
- Engage third-party auditors (subject to confidentiality)
- Review security certifications and audit reports

## 10. Data Breach Notification

### 10.1 Incident Response
Upon becoming aware of a Personal Data breach, Starbox shall:
- Contain and investigate the incident immediately
- Notify Customer within 24 hours of discovery
- Provide detailed incident report within 72 hours
- Assist with breach notification to authorities and Data Subjects

### 10.2 Breach Information
Incident notifications include:
- Nature and scope of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of affected Data Subjects
- Likely consequences of the breach
- Measures taken to address the breach
- Recommendations for Customer action

### 10.3 Cooperation
Starbox will cooperate fully with incident investigations and regulatory inquiries.

## 11. Return and Deletion of Data

### 11.1 End of Processing
Upon termination of services, Starbox shall:
- Return all Personal Data to Customer (within 30 days)
- Delete all copies from systems and backups (within 90 days)
- Provide certification of deletion upon request
- Ensure sub-processors comply with deletion obligations

### 11.2 Data Export
Customer may request data export in commonly used formats:
- JSON structured data export
- CSV format for tabular data
- Native file formats for uploaded documents
- Database dumps for technical teams

### 11.3 Legal Hold Exception
Data may be retained longer if required by:
- Applicable legal or regulatory requirements
- Pending litigation or investigations
- Legitimate archival purposes (anonymized)

## 12. Compliance and Certification

### 12.1 Security Standards
Starbox maintains compliance with:
- ISO 27001 Information Security Management
- SOC 2 Type II (Security, Availability, Confidentiality)
- Swiss cybersecurity framework
- Industry best practices for cloud security

### 12.2 Regular Assessments
- Annual third-party security audits
- Quarterly vulnerability assessments
- Regular penetration testing
- Continuous compliance monitoring

### 12.3 Certification Sharing
Current certifications and audit reports available upon request under appropriate confidentiality agreements.

## 13. Liability and Indemnification

### 13.1 Processor Liability
Starbox is liable for damages caused by processing that:
- Violates GDPR/LPD processor obligations
- Acts outside or contrary to lawful Customer instructions
- Fails to implement appropriate security measures

### 13.2 Limitation of Liability
Subject to applicable law, Starbox's liability is limited to:
- Direct damages only (no consequential damages)
- Maximum amount equal to 12 months of service fees
- Exclusions for force majeure events

### 13.3 Data Protection Indemnity
Starbox will indemnify Customer for regulatory fines resulting from Starbox's breach of this DPA, subject to:
- Customer's prompt notification of proceedings
- Customer's cooperation with defense
- Starbox's control of defense and settlement

## 14. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

### 14.1 Governing Law
This DPA is governed by Swiss law in accordance with the Service Agreement.

### 14.2 Supervisory Authority

### 14.3 Dispute Resolution
Disputes shall be resolved through:
1. Good faith negotiation (30 days)
2. Mediation under Swiss Chamber of Commerce rules
3. Jurisdiction of Geneva courts as final resort

- To reflect changes in processing activities

### 15.2 Regulatory Updates
Starbox may update this DPA to maintain compliance with evolving regulations, with 30 days' notice to Customer.

### 15.3 Termination
This DPA terminates automatically upon:
- Expiration or termination of Service Agreement
- Completion of data return and deletion
- Customer's written termination notice
