## Example Persona: Sarah, Product Manager

**Role:** Mid-level Product Manager  
**Photo:** [Stock photo of professional woman at laptop]

### Demographics
- **Age:** 30-38
- **Location:** Urban/Remote
- **Education:** BS in Computer Science or Business
- **Industry:** SaaS, B2B
- **Company Size:** 50-500 employees (Growth stage startup)
- **Tech Savviness:** High

### Quick Bio
Sarah manages 2-3 products at a fast-growing startup. She's pulled in 10 directions—eng wants clearer specs, sales wants features yesterday, execs want roadmap updates. She's smart and technical but drowning in meetings and context-switching. She wants tools that save time, not add complexity.

### Goals & Motivations
**Primary Goal:** Ship valuable features faster without sacrificing quality

**Secondary Goals:**
- Keep stakeholders aligned without constant meetings
- Make data-driven decisions
- Manage eng and design resources effectively

**Motivations:**
- Wants to be seen as effective and organized
- Craves focus time for strategic thinking
- Frustrated by busywork and tool sprawl

**Quote:**  
> "I spend more time updating Jira and writing Slack summaries than actually thinking about product strategy."

### Pain Points
**Top Pain Point:** Constant context-switching between tools kills productivity

**Other Frustrations:**
- Unclear requirements lead to eng rework
- Hard to track what's actually shipped vs. what's in progress
- Stakeholders ask the same questions repeatedly
- No single source of truth for product decisions

**Current Workarounds:**
- Manually copy-paste updates from Jira to Slack to Google Docs
- Weekly 1-on-1s to keep everyone aligned (time-intensive)
- Personal Notion docs to track decisions (not shared with team)

**Quote:**  
> "I wish I could just say 'Give me a status update' and have it magically pull together everything I need."

### Jobs-to-be-Done
**JTBD Statement:**  
> When I'm preparing for Monday stand-up, I want to quickly pull together what my team shipped last week, so I can confidently report progress without spending an hour digging through Slack and Jira.

**Functional Job:** Generate accurate status reports in <10 minutes  
**Emotional Job:** Feel confident and in control  
**Social Job:** Be seen as organized and communicative leader

### Success Criteria
- Cut status report prep time from 1 hour to 10 minutes
- Zero "I don't know, let me check" moments in exec updates
- Engineering says requirements are clear 90%+ of the time

### Buying Behavior
**Willingness to Pay:** $50-200/month if ROI is clear  
**Budget Authority:** Can expense <$100/month; needs manager approval above  
**Evaluation Criteria:**
1. Works in first 5 minutes (no long onboarding)
2. Integrates with Jira, Slack, Figma
3. Saves at least 5 hours/week

**Deal Breakers:**
- Requires IT setup or admin access
- Doesn't integrate with existing stack
- Forces team to change their workflow
