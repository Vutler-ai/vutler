# Product Discovery Interview Guide

Structured questions for requirements elicitation and product vision discovery.

---

## Interview Principles

1. **Ask "WHY?" at least 3 times** - Surface root causes and true motivations
2. **Listen more than you talk** - Aim for 80/20 ratio
3. **Probe concrete examples** - "Tell me about the last time..." vs. "What do you usually..."
4. **Silence is golden** - Wait 5 seconds after they finish; they often add the best insights
5. **No leading questions** - "How do you feel about..." NOT "Don't you think it's frustrating when..."
6. **Record direct quotes** - Their words reveal unspoken needs

---

## 1. Context Setting (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Build rapport and understand their world

- Tell me about your role and what a typical day looks like.
- What are your primary responsibilities?
- Who do you work with most frequently?
- What tools do you use daily?

---

## 2. Problem Discovery (15 minutes)

**Goal:** Uncover the core problem and its impact

### Current State
- Walk me through [specific workflow/task] from start to finish.
- What makes that difficult/frustrating/time-consuming?
- **WHY?** [Keep asking why to drill down]
- How often does this problem occur?
- What's the impact when it happens?

### Pain Points
- What's the most frustrating part of your day?
- If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about [domain], what would it be?
- What keeps you up at night related to [domain]?
- What problem would you pay to have solved?

### Current Workarounds
- How do you handle this today?
- What have you tried?
- Why doesn't [current solution] work well?
- What's the cost (time/money/sanity) of the current approach?

---

## 3. Jobs-to-be-Done (10 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand the underlying motivation and desired outcome

**Framework:**  
When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome].

**Questions:**
- Walk me through the last time you needed to [do the job].
- What triggered that need?
- What were you trying to accomplish?
- What did success look like?
- What would have made it easier/faster/better?

**Functional Job:**
- What task are you trying to complete?
- What outcome are you trying to achieve?

**Emotional Job:**
- How do you want to feel?
- How do you NOT want to feel?

**Social Job:**
- How do you want to be perceived?
- Who needs to be impressed/informed?

---

## 4. Solution Exploration (10 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand how they imagine solving this

- If you could design the perfect solution, what would it do?
- What's the minimum that would make this worth using?
- What features are must-haves vs. nice-to-haves?
- How would you know this solution is working?
- What would make you stop using it?

**Prioritization:**
- If you could only have ONE feature, what would it be?
- **WHY?**
- What's the second most important?
- **WHY?**

---

## 5. Context & Constraints (10 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand technical, organizational, and personal constraints

### Technical
- What systems does this need to integrate with?
- What data do you already have?
- What are your technical limitations?

### Organizational
- Who else is involved in this process?
- What approvals are needed?
- What's your budget authority?
- What's your timeline?

### Personal
- How comfortable are you with new technology?
- How much time do you have to learn something new?
- What's your preferred way to learn?

---

## 6. Competitive Landscape (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand alternatives and their gaps

- What other solutions have you tried?
- What did you like about them?
- What frustrated you?
- Why did you stop using them (if applicable)?
- If this product didn't exist, what would you do?

---

## 7. Success Definition (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Define measurable success criteria

- How will you measure whether this is working?
- What metric would tell you this is successful?
- What behavior change would you expect to see?
- 6 months from now, how will your day be different?
- What would make you recommend this to a colleague?

---

## 8. Closing (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Capture final insights and next steps

- Is there anything I haven't asked that I should have?
- What's the most important thing I should remember from this conversation?
- What am I missing?
- Who else should I talk to?
- Can I follow up if I have more questions?

---

## Post-Interview Processing

### Immediate Capture (within 30 minutes)
1. Write down direct quotes (their exact words)
2. Note emotional reactions (frustration, excitement, confusion)
3. Identify patterns across interviews
4. List assumptions that need validation

### Analysis
- **Pain Points:** Rank by frequency × severity
- **Jobs-to-be-Done:** Identify primary and secondary jobs
- **Features:** Prioritize by user value (not technical complexity)
- **Metrics:** Define measurable success criteria

### Pattern Recognition (after 3-5 interviews)
- What themes are emerging?
- What's surprising or unexpected?
- Where do users agree/disagree?
- What assumptions are being challenged?

---

## Question Frameworks

### The 5 Whys
Start with a symptom and ask "why" five times to reach root cause.

**Example:**
- *Problem:* "Reports take too long"
- *Why?* "I have to pull data from 3 systems"
- *Why?* "The systems don't talk to each other"
- *Why?* "They were built by different teams"
- *Why?* "No one owned data integration"
- *Why?* "No clear data strategy at the company"

Root cause: Data strategy gap (not a tool problem!)

### The Last Time
"Tell me about the LAST TIME you [did this task]" → Forces concrete examples vs. hypotheticals

### The Magic Wand
"If you could wave a magic wand..." → Reveals ideal state without constraint thinking

### The Extremes
"What's the BEST experience you've had with [similar product]?"  
"What's the WORST experience you've had?"  
→ Reveals boundaries and expectations

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- **"Everyone needs this"** → Probe for specific user segments
- **"We need all the features"** → Force prioritization
- **"Just like [competitor] but better"** → Define "better"
- **"Users want [feature X]"** → Ask for evidence
- **Solution-first thinking** → Redirect to problem discovery
- **Vague metrics** → Push for specific, measurable outcomes

---

## Key Outputs from Interviews

1. **Problem statements** (evidence-based)
2. **User personas** (based on patterns)
3. **Jobs-to-be-Done** (functional, emotional, social)
4. **Feature prioritization** (must-have, should-have, nice-to-have)
5. **Success metrics** (measurable outcomes)
6. **Direct quotes** (for PRD and marketing)
